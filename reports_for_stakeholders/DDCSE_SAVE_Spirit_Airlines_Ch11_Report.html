Spirit emerges as a substantially smaller carrier (~90 aircraft, down from ~200). $795M exit financing. Equity held by former Loyalty Note holders. Unsecured creditors receive nominal recovery. NLR target: <4.0x on smaller debt base.
Investment Thesis & Case Notes
Spirit Airlines represents the definitive case study of ULCC model fragility under financial stress. The company's revenue model — ultra-stripped base fares with aggressive ancillary monetization (bag fees, seat selection, priority boarding) — generated structural EBITDA margins of 8–12% in normal operating conditions. These margins are insufficient to service a $3.3B debt load that was accumulated during COVID-era fleet acquisitions and sale-leaseback transactions at historically low interest rates.

Three compounding failure mechanisms:

(1) M&A Limbo (2022–2024): Spirit spent 26 months in a state of strategic paralysis — first awaiting the Frontier merger outcome, then the JetBlue transaction. During this window, management deferred cost optimization and fleet rationalization, burning cash while competitors (Southwest, Delta, United) invested in network upgrades. The DoJ's JetBlue block in January 2024 crystallized the terminal nature of Spirit's standalone balance sheet.

(2) ULCC Margin Compression: The post-COVID normalization of fuel costs (+38% YoY in 2022), labor agreements (pilots' contract at +34% over 4 years), and maintenance cycle catch-up destroyed ULCC economics. Spirit's EBITDA margin collapsed from ~18% in FY2019 to under 2% in FY2023 on $5.4B revenue. At this EBITDA level, $3.3B in debt produces a NLR of 24.33x — mathematically insolvent.

(3) Cross-Default Cascade: The interconnected covenant structure meant that once the Term Loan B covenant waivers expired in September 2024, acceleration clauses triggered across the EETC tranches (secured by aircraft), the Loyalty Notes (secured by the Free Spirit program), and ultimately all lease obligations. Total at-risk debt in the cascade: $2.44B across the secured stack.

Recovery implications: EETC holders (aircraft-backed, highest seniority) received ~$0.78/$1.00 through aircraft rejections and restructured operating leases. Loyalty Note holders received equity in the reorganized entity at ~$0.32/$1.00. Unsecured creditors recovered less than $0.05/$1.00. Common equity was cancelled.

Sector read-across: Frontier Airlines, Allegiant Air, and Avelo Airlines all carry leverage profiles that warrant surveillance. ULCC business models with Debt/EBITDA above 4.0x and ICR below 2.0x should be classified as structural watchlist credits.
